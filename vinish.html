MARUTHAIYA VINISH

Madurai Veeran (Warrior of Madurai) is a revered Tamil folk guardian deity, particularly popular in southern Tamil Nadu, often depicted holding a sword or aruval on a white horse. A legendary 17th-century hero, he was a skilled warrior appointed by King Thirumalai Nayak to defeat bandits. Veeran later became a protector deity, believed to keep villages safe from disease and danger. The Royal Dancer: He falls in love with Vellaiyammal, a royal dancer. Another version suggests he was caught trying to elope with her from the Meenakshi temple. Betrayal and Punishment: Jealous generals accused Veeran of conspiring with thieves and stalling in protecting the kingdom. King Thirumalai Nayak ordered his limbs to be chopped off. Divine Intervention: The Goddess Meenakshi brought him back to life. Following this, Veeran is said to have beheaded himself and subsequently retired to a cave beneath the Meenakshi Temple.
